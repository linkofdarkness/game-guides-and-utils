## Death

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Death

- When a player dies, they begin to levitate, all their equipment is dropped onto the ground, and their hands reach their neck before having it snapped and dropping dead. The dead player then becomes an observer (see below)
- An observer will see the map through a greyish fog and can only communicate with other observers, though they can still hear the living (which may sound incorrectly muffled due to a bug), including the global chat radio. Observers will be able to phase through all forms of doors, even if they are locked. Ghosts will ignore dead players. Motion Sensors will be triggered if an observer walks past them. Observers can sprint infinitely, unlike regular players which run out of breath after a few seconds and have to "recharge" (unless the "Sprint duration" difficulty setting is set to infinite)
- Observers cannot pick up or interact with equipment or any other investigation-sensitive items (such as bones or cursed possessions). However, they can still pick up decorative items found around the map (dishes, notebooks, shoes, etc)
- If a living player draws a Tarot Card labeled The High Priestess, one dead player will randomly be chosen to be revived. The dead player will reappear at the location of their Dead Body (even if they were outside the investigation before being resurrected). Otherwise, the observer will remain as one until the game ends. The Monkey Paw can also be used to revive an existing dead player, but with a 50% chance of killing the wisher at the same time
- If a player dies and revives in the same hunt, they cannot be killed again for that hunt (i.e. the player cannot die twice in the same hunt)
- If all players in the round die, the game will end shortly after the last player returns as an observer

## Experience

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Experience

- Each player starts off at level 1 with 0 XP, and earns XP through finishing contracts. The experience required to level up increases progressively, and the level is capped at 9,999. Once level 100 has been reached, the player can choose to Prestige, which will wipe out their level, money, and equipment, in exchange for a new badge and title for the ID Card. The maximum Prestige level is 3,999
- The ID Card lists the number of levels the player has and the amount of XP required to reach the next level. The amount of XP earned each contract is displayed on the debriefing screen
- Players may gain XP from the following sources:
- To get the additional Perfect Investigation Bonus of 50 XP, the player must identify the correct ghost, complete all 3 objectives, collect the bone, and fill the journal with only unique media
- The amount of experience gained from identifying the ghost, completing optional objectives, recorded media, and any investigation bonus(es) is then multiplied by the difficulty multiplier:
- Intermediate: 2x
- Professional: 3x
- Custom: 0x - 15x depending on settings
- If the player does not identify the correct ghost on a custom difficulty, their rewards will be rounded down to the nearest default difficulty (e.g. from 7x to 6x, which is the Insanity multiplier). Difficulty multipliers below 1x are not affected
- If the player is dead when the investigation ends, the difficulty multiplier is not applied if it is higher than 1x, and their experience gained is halved
- If the resulting EXP is not an integer value, it is then rounded down to the nearest integer
- Players will unlock more equipment, difficulty levels, and maps as they progress
- Prestige level doesn't change the amount of EXP for level up

## Journal

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Journal

- The Journal can be opened by pressing the Journal button, or grabbing the book from the belt on VR. Upon opening it for the first time, players will be greeted with a contents page outlining each section of the book. Several bookmarks are also visible at the top of the journal, allowing players to quickly switch between sections
- This section outlines the fundamentals and basic game mechanics in Phasmophobia, including subsections on sanity and tools
- This section provides information on payment bonuses, cursed possessions, and gathering evidence
- The Overview page is a single page detailing information unique to the specific case a player is on. From here, players can view:
- The ghost's name
- Who it responds to
- Any collected keys
- All optional objectives
- This allows players to check objectives and case information on-the-go without needing to constantly run back to the truck

## Sanity

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Sanity

- There are two main readings of sanity in the game: the individual sanity of each player, and the average team sanity of all players. These values can be read at any time on the truck's sanity monitor or roughly visualized on the watch
- At the start of the contract, once an exit door has been opened, players' sanities will begin to be able to drop. Sanity affects things like interactions, ghost events, hunts, and flickering lights; lower average sanity results in increasing relative frequency of such paranormal activity, though as there is a high degree of randomness in these occurrences to begin with, this may not always be readily apparent over the course of an investigation. Individual sanities are not taken into account for these effects in most cases, meaning that a player with a high sanity level going inside may still face more aggressive behavior from the ghost if their partners staying outside all have near zero sanity
- Dead players' sanities are not included when calculating the average team sanity, but still exist in the background. Dead players are no longer affected by sanity drain except for the 15% deduction from other players dying, the 16% deduction from the Summoning Circle's candles, and some ghost abilities
- All ghosts have a specific hunt sanity threshold below which they will be able to attempt a hunt. The Banshee is the only ghost which uses its target's sanity to decide whether to hunt, while other effects still use average sanity
- When a player reaches 0% sanity, nothing noteworthy occurs, save for the completion of the "Reach 0 sanity" Daily Task if available and not yet completed that day. Sanity cannot go into the negatives
- Whether passive sanity drain is applied depends on the status of the lights in the room. In most rooms, sanity drain will be prevented by turning on all of their "main lights", these generally being ceiling light sources controlled by wall switches, rather than lamps or televisions, of which will still provide visibility when turned on but will not block sanity drain. In larger rooms with multiple dark spots (e.g. most hallways in Sunny Meadows Mental Institution), sanity drain will not be blocked entirely even with the lights on, but will instead be reduced to 80% of the normal rate. Additionally, being near a firelight will reduce sanity drain by a variable percentage based on the tier of the item, even if the room's lights are off
- Examples of situations that will not prevent passive sanity drain include:
- Holding a lit flashlight of any type
- Standing in front of an active computer, even though it technically counts as a light source
- Turning up the game's brightness
- Protections against passive sanity drain do not apply to other types of drains
- The table below lists the drain rates when the "main lights" are off:
- 1x rate of decay for Amateur
- 1.5x rate of decay for Intermediate
- 2x rate of decay for Professional, Nightmare, and Insanity
- 0x - 2x rate of decay for Custom

## Setup Phase

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Setup_Phase

- The duration of the setup phase depends on the difficulty:
- Countdown of the setup phase is shown on the timer in the truck above the computer, and will only begin when any entryway to the building is opened
- During the setup phase, sanity decay due to being in darkness is reduced by roughly 30%, and sanity cannot go below 50%. Additionally, the ghost cannot initiate a hunt on its own, regardless of its hunt sanity threshold or any abilities it may have. Other than these conditions, the setup phase does not affect the ghost's behaviour or other game mechanics. When the time runs out, the ghost will be allowed to initiate a hunt, depending on the ghost type and the players' average sanity, and the sanity drain rate will increase to normal
- The setup phase can be ended early by activating or using any cursed possession. There is currently a bug where the Ouija Board cancels the setup phase only if it recognizes a valid phrase instead of upon activation

## Temperature

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Temperature

- The temperature of a room, both indoors and outdoors, is dependent on the weather. Each weather type will have a specific range of temperatures:
- Before the investigation area/building is entered, all rooms will be at the maximum weather temperature. If the difficulty has the fuse box turned on at start, such as in Amateur, all indoors rooms will start at instead. The ghost will not affect the temperature of its current room until an exit door is opened
- When a player is in a room that is at or below , they will start emitting a visible breath
- After an exit door is opened, temperatures of all rooms will change dynamically:
- The temperature of a room increases at a rate of 0.025°C (0.045°F) per second up until:
- the maximum weather temperature if the room is outdoors
- the minimum weather temperature if the room is indoors, the temperature was below the minimum weather temperature, and the fuse box is off
- if the room is indoors and the fuse box is on
- The temperature of a room decreases at a rate of 0.1°C (0.18°F) per second, and at 1/3 of this rate once it is below the minimum weather temperature until:
- the maximum weather temperature if the room is outdoors, or if the fuse box is off
- if the ghost is present in the room, or if the room is Cooler Room on Nell's Diner and the fuse box is on
- The temperature of a room remains at its current value if the room is indoors, the fuse box is off, and the temperature is between the minimum weather temperature and the maximum weather temperature
- The temperature of a room can be read by a thermometer. The value of the reading will fluctuate upwards from the actual room's temperature by a maximum of 3°C (5.4°F) with a tier I or II thermometer, and by a maximum of 2°C (3.6°F) with a tier III thermometer
- When lit, Camp Woodwind's campfire instantly raises the temperature of its "room" to , even if the ghost is in that room. The campfire itself will usually exceed . This does not apply to Maple Lodge Campsite's campfires, which is likely a bug
- The Hantu's speed during a hunt is affected by the temperature of the room it is currently in. The colder the room, the faster it can move

## Weather

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Weather

- There are currently eight types of weather. Seven among which one will be randomly chosen per game with equal chance, and an additional eighth type that has a 2% chance to replace the chosen weather. Depending on what weather type is present, players may experience auditory and visual obstructions
- Weather is grouped into four groups of temperatures. From warmest to coldest:
- Clear, Fog, Blood Moon
- Light Rain, Heavy Rain, Windy
- This can affect how the player finds the ghost; warmer weather means that the average room temperature where the ghost is will be slightly higher than in colder weather
- The effects of weather are suppressed while indoors: noise disruption (save for the occasional roar of thunder) is quieted, and the visual effects of fog and precipitation do not make their way inside. However, weather can play a major part in Camp Woodwind and Maple Lodge Campsite, precisely as these maps are mainly played outside
