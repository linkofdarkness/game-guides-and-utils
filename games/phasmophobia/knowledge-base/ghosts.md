## Aswang

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Aswang

- Evidence: D.O.T.S. Projector, Freezing Temperatures, Ghost Writing
- Journal strength: Will accelerate faster with line-of-sight than any other ghost.; Can sometimes hunt with zero grace period.
- Journal weakness: Cannot kill players in official hiding spots.; Slower hunt base speed.
- Current behavior facts:
- The hunt will instantly end if the Aswang detects and reaches a player inside an available official hiding spot (such as a closet or a locker) used correctly, meaning it is impossible for players to be killed while hiding. However, if the Aswang were to end a hunt this way, then the ghost will place a waypoint directly at the player's location at the start of the next hunt and move to it, even during the grace period

## Banshee

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Banshee

- Evidence: D.O.T.S. Projector, Ghost Orb, Ultraviolet
- Journal strength: Focuses on the same player until that player is killed or has left the game
- Journal weakness: Cannot kill players other than the target if the target is inside; Can produce unique screams on the parabolic microphone and the sound recorder
- Ability summary: Performs singing ghost events more often than other ghost types; Frequently roams to target player outside of hunts
- Current behavior facts:
- At the start of the game, a player is randomly selected as the hunt target. If the hunt target leaves the game or dies, a new hunt target will be selected. The new target is not random: the Banshee will select the first alive player in the list (established by the order the players joined the lobby) that is not the previous target, unless there is only one player remaining
- The Banshee's activity is based on the hunt target's sanity
- When performing a ghost event, the Banshee has a 46.67% chance of choosing the singing event instead of 20% for most other ghosts
- If the hunt target is the target of a singing event and causes the Banshee to disappear prematurely (by going near it), then their sanity will decrease by 15%, instead of the usual 10%
- Like other ghosts, who the Banshee targets for a ghost event is random and based on distance; being the hunt target by itself does not increase their chance of being the ghost event target
- If the hunt target is inside the investigation area, the Banshee has a 2/3 chance to travel to that player while in D.O.T.S. state
- Contrary to most other ghosts, the Banshee is able to perform long roams while its current room is not the favourite room, and has a 50% chance to perform long roams instead of the regular chances
- When the Banshee performs a short roam, if the hunt target is inside the investigation area, it has a 2/3 (67%) chance of triggering its ability which makes it place a waypoint at the target's location, and travel to it. Unlike the Wraith's and the Phantom's abilities, this will not create any EMF reading. The ability fails if the short roam conditions are not fulfilled (e.g. player not on same floor, pathing distance greater than 150% direct distance)
- When a Parabolic Microphone or a Sound Recorder is used, the Banshee has a 33% chance of making 1 of its 20 unique screams instead of a regular paranormal sound
- The Banshee's hunt threshold is 50%, but it only checks the target's sanity when trying to hunt, rather than the average sanity like other ghost types, meaning that only the target needs to be at or below 50% sanity for it to attempt to hunt. As a result, the Banshee can appear to start hunting from as high as 87% average sanity, or as low as 12% average sanity, on the Sanity Monitor, depending on the target's current sanity and number of other players

## Dayan

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Dayan

- Evidence: EMF Level 5, Ghost Orb, Spirit Box
- Journal strength: Moves quicker during hunts and hunts sooner when a player moves near the ghost
- Journal weakness: Moves slower during hunts and hunts later when a player stands still near the ghost
- Current behavior facts:
- When a player is within 10 metres of the Dayan, whether on the same floor or not, the ghost will have a modified hunt sanity threshold and hunt speed depending on further factors:
- If the closest player is still, the threshold is decreased to 45% and the ghost will have a fixed speed of 1.2 m/s
- If the closest player is walking, the threshold is increased to 65% and the ghost will have a fixed speed of 2.25 m/s
- If there is no player within 10 metres of the Dayan, it will have the standard threshold of 50% and follow standard ghost speeds with one particularity: if the Dayan has line-of-sight with a player within 10 metres, the speed increase from continuous line-of-sight adds in the background, and will only be applied once it is no longer near any player
- The Dayan can only be a female ghost and will use a female name and ghost model

## Demon

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Demon

- Evidence: Freezing Temperatures, Ghost Writing, Ultraviolet
- Journal strength: Lower hunt cooldown than other ghosts; Higher hunt sanity threshold; Smudging prevents hunts for shorter
- Journal weakness: Increased effective crucifix range
- Ability summary: Rare chance to hunt at any sanity percentage
- Current behavior facts:
- The Demon has an ability that allows it to automatically start a hunt, regardless of current average sanity. There is no particular behaviour or condition (such as requiring line-of-sight with a player) that affects if it activates or not; if it decides to use its ability, it will hunt. This hunt can be blocked by normal prevention methods
- Because the Demon's ability hunt is governed by the "ability phase", these hunts occur much less frequently than hunts derived from being below its sanity threshold. Additionally, as this is not a "proper" hunt attempt, the Demon can attempt to use its ability when it is unable to hunt (during the setup phase on Amateur and Intermediate difficulties, when it is affected by Incense, or when a hunt has just ended and it is on cooldown). However, it will fail and there will be no hunt
- The Demon has a normal hunt sanity threshold of 70%, instead of the standard 50%. This does not affect and is independent from its hunt ability, although the hunt does not differ in any way
- Demons have a minimum cooldown of 20 seconds between hunts, instead of the usual 25 seconds
- Smudging a Demon will prevent hunts for only 60 seconds, as opposed to the standard 90 seconds
- As a weakness, the effective range of a crucifix is 50% larger than the standard range:
- 4.5 metres instead of 3 for Tier I
- 6 metres instead of 4 for Tier II
- 7.5 metres instead of 5 for Tier III

## Deogen

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Deogen

- Evidence: D.O.T.S. Projector, Ghost Writing, Spirit Box
- Journal strength: Always knows where players are during a hunt; Moves at a high speed with no player nearby
- Journal weakness: Significantly slows down when near its target; Has a lower hunt sanity threshold
- Ability summary: Can produce a unique response through the Spirit Box
- Current behavior facts:
- If the player asks any question through the Spirit Box while standing within 1 metre of the Deogen, it has a 33% chance with each question to produce a unique response of constant heavy bull-like breathing, instead of a regular response
- The Deogen is more likely to interact with doors compared to most other ghosts (30% chance instead of the 25% chance for a standard ghost)
- The Deogen's hunt sanity threshold is 40%, instead of the usual 50%
- During a hunt, the Deogen always has line-of-sight with all players on the map; in other words, it does not use the "roaming" mechanic. When the hunt begins, the Deogen will pick the nearest player (by straight-line distance) to move towards. It will focus that player for 10 seconds before being able to check and repick the nearest target every 5-10 seconds throughout the hunt. One can also use Incense to stun the Deogen temporarily and force it to change its target when the smudge effect runs out
- The Deogen moves at a speed based on its pathfinding distance to the target player and does not speed up due to line-of-sight:
- Above 6 metres: 3 m/s
- Below 6 metres: pathfinding distance × 0.15 m/s
- Minimum limit: 0.4 m/s
- During an incense's blinding effect, the Deogen's speed is set at 0.4 m/s if the difficulty uses a 50%, 75%, or 100% ghost speed, and at 1.6 m/s if the difficulty uses a 125% or 150% ghost speed
- During a hunt, the Deogen flickers and is on average invisible for shorter and visible for longer compared to most other ghosts:

## Goryo

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Goryo

- Evidence: D.O.T.S. Projector, EMF Level 5, Ultraviolet
- Journal strength: Usually shows itself passing through D.O.T.S on video camera only with no players nearby

- The Goryo has a 2/3 chance to enter the D.O.T.S state from the roaming state instead of a 1/3 chance for other ghosts
- When choosing a favourite room state, the Goryo has a 10% chance to enter a D.O.T.S state instead, making it the only ghost that can enter a D.O.T.S state while the roaming state is disabled due to "The Hermit" tarot card or the Monkey Paw's 'trap wish'
- If there is a player inside the investigation area, the Goryo's D.O.T.S state will make it move towards a random waypoint within 1 metre (instead of 2 metres for other ghosts), and that waypoint must be within the favourite room

## Kormos

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Kormos

- Evidence: Ghost Orb, Spirit Box, Ultraviolet
- Journal strength: Higher hunt sanity threshold when nearby players sprint outside hunts.; Can detect a player's footsteps from far away during a hunt.
- Journal weakness: Nearly blind and cannot see still players or from afar.
- Current behavior facts:
- The Kormos cannot perform mist form and chasing ghost events
- The Kormos has a hunt sanity threshold of 50% by default. It is increased to 70% if a player sprints in the same room as it
- By default, the Kormos does not have line-of-sight. It can only achieve it if the player moves within 5 metres with no obstacles (e.g. furniture, walls). When having no line-of-sight, it will kill players on the same floor in the investigation area by simply getting within 1.5 metres of them, ignoring obstacles
- Contrary to other ghosts, the Kormos can update its detection waypoint before reaching the previous one, after a timer of 5-10 seconds
- The Kormos has a base speed of 1.7 m/s. Its current speed is multiplied by 1.3 if it travels to a detection waypoint while having no line-of-sight
- Additionally to the detection from voice and electronics, the Kormos has a heightened hearing that allows it to detect footsteps sounds emitted by nearby players. Other types of sounds such as the player's breathing or an object throw do not count. The game stores only one sound per player
- Every second starting from the beginning of the hunt, including the grace period, the Kormos will use a "hearing pulse" that first clears a list of detected sounds before checking again for recently emitted footstep sounds. The check is successful if a sound (lasting approximately 0.2-0.4 seconds) overlaps with the hearing pulse. A sound will however be ignored if it has been emitted outside of the ranges listed in the table below, if the sound is not located on the same floor as the ghost, or if the pathing distance between the ghost and the sound's location is strictly greater than 1.5 times the range. Valid sounds are added to the list of detected sounds
- If the Kormos was not already travelling to a detection waypoint or LoS waypoint, it will read the list of detected sounds and select the one with the highest priority, or the nearest if there are multiple of the same priority. A timer of 5-10 seconds will then start and a detection waypoint will be placed on the player's exact location, continuously updating until the next hearing pulse
- If the list of detected sounds contains a sound after the timer expires or after the Kormos reaches the detection waypoint, it will set a new detection waypoint at the sound's location rather than the player's exact location then restart the timer. Otherwise, it will simply return to a wandering phase (after reaching the detection waypoint if it hasn't reached it yet)

## Mare

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Mare

- Evidence: Ghost Orb, Ghost Writing, Spirit Box
- Journal strength: Hunts at a higher sanity threshold in an unlit room
- Journal weakness: Hunts at a lower sanity threshold in a lit room
- Ability summary: Turns off lights and breaks lightbulbs more often; May turn off a light immediately after a player turns one on; Wanders more often when lights are on
- Current behavior facts:
- Mares cannot interact with lights to turn them on, and they are more likely to choose turning off lights as an interaction. They also cannot perform the light flickering interaction (the one that specifically leaves an EMF reading at a light switch as opposed to the "atmospheric" light flickering)
- The Mare does not have any affinity with computer monitors, televisions, Bleasdale Farmhouse's motion activated lights, and the fuse box, and can turn them on or off like most other ghosts. The Mare also does not have any affinity with fire sources and can still light them up
- When performing a ghost event, the Mare has a 11.11% chance of choosing the light-shattering event instead of 6.67% for other ghosts
- When the lights are on in its current room, the Mare's long roam chances are set to 50%
- Contrary to most other ghosts, the Mare is able to perform long roams while its current room is not the favourite room, provided it is lit
- A Mare has a modified hunt sanity threshold depending on whether the room it is currently in is lit. If the lights are turned off, the hunt sanity threshold is increased to 60%, and if the lights are turned on, the threshold is decreased to 40%. This applies even if the fuse box is turned off or broken, but not if the room's lights are destroyed. Other sources of light, such as brought-in equipment and Firelights, do not affect this threshold

- The Moroi's speed is dependent on the average sanity, and will change during a hunt according to the current average sanity. ## Onryo

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Onryo

- Evidence: Freezing Temperatures, Ghost Orb, Spirit Box
- Journal strength: Can sometimes hunt at any sanity after blowing out a flame; Blows out fiery objects (candles, campfire) more often with more dead people
- Journal weakness: Presence of flames prevents the ghost from hunting
- Current behavior facts:
- The Onryo's flame blowing frequency increases the more dead players there are:
- Contrary to the journal's description, flames do not make the Onryo more likely to leave a room
- The Onryo has a hunt sanity threshold of 60% by default. When it is within 4 metres of a flame, this threshold is decreased to 40%
- If the Onryo attempts a hunt within 4 metres of any type of flame (e.g. firelight, igniter, campfire, etc.), it will blow out the flame instead, and the hunt will fail. This does not restart the hunt cooldown timer, unlike a crucifix. If both a crucifix and a flame are within range, the flame will take priority over the crucifix for preventing the hunt
- The Onryo's hunt attempts also override a flame's individual cooldowns, making it capable of blowing out a firelight within 30 seconds of its first lighting, or within 20 seconds of its subsequent lightings. Note that a tier III firelight's current cooldown is paused if it is turned off by the player or an Onryo's hunt attempt

## Yurei

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Yurei

- Evidence: D.O.T.S. Projector, Freezing Temperatures, Ghost Orb
- Journal weakness: Will be temporarily confined to its room after smudging
- Ability summary: May randomly shut a door and deduct nearby players' sanity
- Current behavior facts:

- When opening or closing a hinged/sliding door with a creaking sound, it will always use an amplitude of 1 instead of 0.2-1 for other ghosts

- Outside of its ability, the Yurei doesn't have an increased chance to interact with doors
