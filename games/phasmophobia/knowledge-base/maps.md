## 10 Ridgeview Court

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/10_Ridgeview_Court

- Mapsize: Small
- Unlocklevel: 2
- Floors: 3
- Rooms: 6 (2F) 9 (1F) 1 (B)
- Exits: 1
- Faucets: 5
- Videofeeds: 1
- Keys: Front Door Key, Car Key

### Layout And Hiding
- Ridgeview has four bedrooms, four toilets, a garage and a basement, among other rooms
- The "Master Bedroom" consists of the bedroom itself, the attached bathroom, and the closet that can be accessed through said bathroom
- The small hallway leading to "Master Bedroom" is considered part of "Kitchen"
- The Upstairs Hallway includes both the main hallway that turns a corner and leads to "Boys Bedroom" and "Bathroom" and the straight hallway partially separated by a wall that leads to "Teen Boys Bedroom", "Girls Bedroom", and "Upstairs Toilet"
- In order to fully prevent passive sanity drain in the Foyer, the light switch that turns on the lamp outside at the entrance door has to be turned on as well
- There is a bug where the computer in Hallway needs to be turned on to prevent passive sanity drain in that room
- "Basement" is a room that allows short roams to another floor to be completed
- See Third-party tools for a list of detailed map viewers that players may use
- There are 10 "sanctioned" hiding spots in Ridgeview:
- Living Room, behind stacked storage crates in corner by fireplace
- Garage, lockers near entrance door
- Garage, lockers along far wall

### Cursed Possession Locations
- The Haunted Mirror spawns above the lamp near the stairs in the Hallway

- The Ouija Board spawns on the shelf in the Utility

## Asylum

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Asylum

- Mapsize: Large
- Unlocklevel: 5
- Floors: 2
- Rooms: 80 (1F) 39 (B); 119 (Total); 111 (Possible ghost rooms)
- Exits: 1
- Faucets: 43
- Videofeeds: 15
- Keys: Main Door Key

### Layout And Hiding
- The Asylum had 119 labeled rooms, the most on any map, while subdividing these into smaller, individual rooms would result in over 200 rooms. A large proportion of these are bedrooms, with other rooms such as a dentist room, conference rooms, canteens, barbers, storages, and more
- This map's weather was always set to Clear Skies
- All bedrooms are labeled starting with "Size Bedroom #", where Size is "Two", "Four" or "Six" and represents the number of beds in that bedroom, while # identifies the specific bedroom (e.g., the second four-bed room would be called Four Bedroom 2). Only the left/right wings of the first floor have bedrooms. The bedrooms fully occupy both outer rows of each wing (one row on the same side as the truck and the other on the opposite side)
- "Two Bedroom #" rooms are the smallest bedrooms nearest to the middle of the building. Each of the 4 rows of bedrooms has a room called "Two Bedroom #"
- "Four Bedroom ##" rooms are all on the same side of the map as the truck (at the bottom of the wings in the site map diagram)
- "Six Bedroom ##" rooms are all on the opposite side of the map as the truck (at the top of the wings in the site map diagram)
- There are no rooms in the map whose names begin with "One", "Three", or "Five"
- The 4 rooms named "Day Room #" are all found on the first floor
- Because the map used to be rotated 180 degrees, all rooms with "Right" and "Left" labels are currently oppositely labeled
- All hallways and stairways cannot be chosen as a ghost room, meaning that players do not have to check for Ghost Orbs in these rooms. The rooms named Telephones and Lobby can still be ghost rooms
- See Third-party tools for a list of detailed map viewers that players may use
- There are 2 sets of lockers in Asylum. These are always free to hide in, even on Nightmare difficulty. Players usually hide behind furniture or in rooms due to the lack of lockers. Alternatively, you can also hide in the bathroom stalls or in storage as a last resort

### Cursed Possession Locations
- All cursed possessions would spawn in the Lobby

## Brownstone High School

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Brownstone_High_School

- Mapsize: Large
- Unlocklevel: 8
- Floors: 2
- Rooms: 34 (1F) 30 (2F); 64 (Total); 58 (Possible ghost rooms)
- Exits: 5
- Faucets: 36
- Videofeeds: 15
- Keys: Main Door

### Layout And Hiding
- Brownstone High School has one front and one side yard (separated from each other), about three dozen classrooms, eight bathrooms, a lecture hall, a cafeteria, and a gymnasium complete with two locker rooms. The entire building is approximately 85 by 55 meters
- Brownstone High School features five exit doors. Due to the large distance between the closest entrance and the truck, it may be desirable to stage gear close to the building before entering. The rear exit doors lead onto a outdoor yard featuring an exterior basketball court. It is considered a safe zone, therefore preventing passive sanity drain
- All hallways on both floors cannot be chosen as a favourite room, meaning Ghost Orbs do not need to be checked for in these hallways. The Lobby can be chosen as a favourite room, however
- The four Science Classrooms at the back of the building (two downstairs and two upstairs) each consist of two smaller sub-rooms: the classroom with chairs and the science lab with large metal tables
- There are four pairs of bathrooms on the map, with one pair per side per floor:
- All male bathrooms are on the left, and all female bathrooms are on the right
- Each pair consists of a student's bathroom (labelled boys/girls) and a teacher's bathroom (labelled male/female). The teacher's bathroom is behind the "regular" bathroom (when looking towards the back)
- The bathrooms are additionally labelled "upstairs" or "downstairs" respectively
- Classrooms numbered 20 and above are all located on the 2nd floor. The rest, classrooms 1-19, are downstairs
- The upstairs hallway is divided into three separate rooms by the game. The game calls each of these 6 hallway rooms a "Corridor" (rather than a "Hallway")
- In addition to doors and windows, fingerprints can also appear on bathroom mirrors
- The following rooms count as dim rooms and hence will drain sanity with 80% of the rate, even with all room lights being on:

### Cursed Possession Locations
- All cursed possessions spawn in the Lobby or in the immediate vicinity in the hallway
- The Voodoo Doll spawn on a bench in the end of the Lobby
- The Summoning Circle spawns in the end of the Lobby
- The Ouija Board spawns behind the left column in the Lobby
- The Haunted Mirror spawns behind the right column in the Lobby

### Layout And Hiding
- Camp Woodwind is analogous with 6 Tanglewood Drive for number of accessible areas (rooms vs. tents), but only possesses one open area pathway that can be circumnavigated around the main tent. This causes the map to feel almost unnervingly small and the ghost to be roaming very close to the entrance either way
- There are four tents, three clustered near the Picnic Area and one near the Bathroom, and a campfire. In addition, there is a shed-like area towards the back, referred to as "Lovers Bench". - For faster ghosts, looping around the only pathway may not be a suitable option due to its small size. Always prioritize smudging and hiding
- When lit, the campfire does not affect sanity drain, but maintains the temperature in the Campfire at , even if the ghost is in that room, provided the weather is not Snow

- There are 7 "sanctioned" hiding spots on Camp Woodwind:
- Campfire, behind multiple barrels next to Games Tent
- Campfire, behind multiple cooler boxes next to Blue Tent
- Picnic Area, behind cooler boxes next to White Tent
- Yellow Tent, behind several sleeping bags on the right section
- Kids' Area, two small tents (orange and cyan)

## Prison

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Prison

- Mapsize: Medium
- Unlocklevel: 11
- Floors: 2
- Rooms: 12 (2F) 19 (1F); 31 (Total); 29 (Possible ghost rooms)
- Exits: 3
- Faucets: 6 (2F) 10 (1F)
- Videofeeds: 5 (2F) 6 (1F)
- Keys: Main Door Key

### Layout And Hiding
- Prison contains a variety of rooms, including two prison blocks containing all the jail cells, offices of various types, an infirmary room, visitation rooms, and more. Though slightly smaller than Brownstone High School, the Prison can be just as dangerous: a safe location during a hunt may be a great distance away, demanding a long run, compounded with the ghost's favourite room being able to be located in many areas that have only one or two routes of escape, including congested catwalks and entire cell block floors. Incense may be essential for survival in these situations, especially against ghosts with a speed advantage
- Many parts of the building have light switches located in nearby separate rooms, requiring players to head away from them to manipulate the location's lighting. This can occasionally cause confusion with the ghost manipulating switches that illuminate other rooms
- There are exit doors at the end of both prison blocks that lead into an outdoor courtyard, providing an alternative access route. This outside area also prevents passive sanity drain
- Each cell block consists of 5 distinct rooms. The first floor consists of three rooms: the left row of prison cells, the right row of prison cells including the small control room, and the open area in between, which includes the area up to the inner steel gates. The second floor consists of two rooms, split evenly down the middle with each row of cells in one room
- The computer keyboards in the cell block control rooms control the lights for the entire cell block
- The two upstairs rooms with hospital beds (separated by a single door) are considered by the game as one room called "Infirmary"
- The upstairs room named "Infirmary Hallway" consists of 4 smaller "sub-rooms", including the small hallway leading to "Cafeteria Security" and the small room that may spawn a fuse box
- The rooms called Main Hallway and Office Hallway cannot be chosen as a ghost room
- The light switch for the Main Hallway is found in the Central Control room
- The following rooms count as dark rooms and hence will drain sanity with 80% of the rate, even with all room lights being on:
- Entrance, Main Hallway, Cafeteria and Office Hallway
- See Third-party tools for a list of detailed map viewers that players may use

### Cursed Possession Locations
- All cursed possessions will spawn in the entrance hallway
- The Haunted Mirror spawns under a row of chairs
- The Summoning Circle spawns at the end of the hallway
- The Tarot Cards spawn in the blue crate
- The Music Box spawns in the black crate
- The Voodoo Doll spawns on the table between the crates
- The Ouija Board spawns on the floor behind the table with the crates
- The Monkey Paw spawns besides the blue crate on the table

## Sunny Meadows Mental Institution

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Sunny_Meadows_Mental_Institution

- Mapsize: Large
- Unlocklevel: 17
- Floors: 2
- Rooms: 69 (Total); 1F: 44; B: 22; Staircases: 3
- Exits: 1
- Faucets: 22
- Videofeeds: 8
- Keys: Main Door Key

### Layout And Hiding
- Sunny Meadows contains 69 rooms, split into 5 wings and across 2 floors, as well as an outdoor courtyard. The wings are:
- Courtyard Wing: Built around a large courtyard, this wing contains amenities for patients and visitors as well as staff facilities
- Male Wing: This wing contains several bedrooms, a dormitory, a common bathroom, as well as a day room for the male patients of the institution
- Female Wing: This wing contains several bedrooms, a dormitory, a common bathroom, as well as a day room for the female patients of the institution
- Hospital Wing: This wing contains several common hospital rooms as well as an operating room and a macabre morgue, still reeking with the remains of its past residents
- Restricted Wing: This wing was presumably where the most aggressive or unfortunate patients received their "treatments" under the form of brutal torture, containing several cells filled with blood and gore
- A labeled map, albeit one that is weathered and altogether difficult to read, is provided in the Entrance Corridor
- The room names Servant Room 1 and Servant Room 2 are swapped on this map from what is actually defined in-game
- Certain rooms contain old bell-type alarm clocks and metronomes which the ghost might interact with
- The crosses found hanging on the walls in various parts of the building can be turned upside down by the ghost, which will emit a loud scratching sound. Finding a cross that has been turned upside down (except for the ones in the Chapel) is a strong sign that the ghost is nearby. Crosses cannot be flipped back upright once they have been interacted with
- In general, the main hallways are littered with props and obstructions; running in a straight line without bumping into something is generally not feasible
- The ghost cannot perform a ghost event on players that are standing in the following rooms:

### Cursed Possession Locations
- All the cursed possessions are located in the Chapel: the Monkey Paw at the foot of the large crucifix, and five other possessions arranged around the Summoning Circle in the middle
