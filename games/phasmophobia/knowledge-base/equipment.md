## Crucifix

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Crucifix

- Price: $30
- Maxamount: 2
- Uses: Depends
- Starter: No
- Tier I Level: 7
- Tier Ii Level: 34
- Tier Iii Level: 80

### Tier I
- Description: Just two scraps of wood tied together. The shape alone seems to be enough to ward off some paranormal entities.
- Use: This crucifix can be held or placed to stop the ghost from initiating a single attack.; Make sure to keep it placed near any activity you may find.
- Stats: Range (m): 3; Uses: 1; Placeable; Consumable

### Tier Ii
- Description: Upgraded from wood to cast iron, this cross provides a much more reliable defence.
- Use: A metal crucifix that can be used to block two attacks, with a larger range.; Remember to keep it in close proximity to any activity you come across.
- Stats: Range (m): 4; Uses: 2; Placeable; Consumable

### Tier Iii
- Description: This ornate crucifix has been adorned with gold and silver linings. These intricate designs have been proven to increase defense against other worldly beings.
- Use: This crucifix has an even larger range than its previous version. Additionally, an unused crucifix can prevent a cursed hunt, consuming both uses.
- Stats: Range (m): 5; Uses: 2; Prevents one cursed hunt; Placeable; Consumable

### Mechanics
- 200px|thumb|right|The tier II crucifix's effective range shown while placing it.]]
- A crucifix can be held in the player's hand, thrown, or placed; the crucifix works in all three situations. Holding the placing button will show the default effective range for the crucifix; this is a translucent sphere with a radius depending on its tier level (even if the ghost has a different effective range):
- Tier I: 3 metres
- Tier II: 4 metres
- Tier III: 5 metres
- When the ghost attempts to initiate a hunt within the crucifix's range, the hunt will fail to begin, and a charge will be used. More precisely, the hunt fails if both the ghost's feet and the ghost's head (located 1.5 metres above the floor) are within the range. The ghost is then teleported back to its favourite room
- Each time a crucifix successfully prevents a hunt, an EMF Level 2 reading is created, with a 33% chance for this to be EMF Level 5 if it is one of the ghost's types of evidence. The ghost must also wait a minimum of 25 seconds (20 seconds for a Demon) before being able to attempt other hunts
- For the Demon, the crucifix's effective range is increased by 50% for each tier:

- Stats: Range (m): 5; Spotlight: Narrow; Placeable; Electronic

### Tier Ii
- Description: This infrared motion sensor was adapted heavily to project light. It’s small and compact and can stick to most surfaces.
- Use: This small projector emits a medium sized sphere of light. Place it on the floor for the best coverage of small to medium-sized rooms.
- Stats: Range (m): 2.5; Area Light; Placeable; Electronic

### Tier Iii
- Description: A motorized projector with three lenses for a wide coverage of light.
- Use: For the best coverage, place this projector on a wall or upright surface, stand nearby, and watch as it scans the environment.; To increase coverage even more, place two projectors opposite each other to fill entire rooms with infrared light!
- Stats: Range (m): 7; Spotlight: Wide; Placeable; Scanning; Electronic

### Mechanics
- The D.O.T.S Projector can be placed on a wall or floor similar to a motion sensor. It emits a matrix of bright green laser points in a small area from the projector. Tier I D.O.T.S Projector additionally can be held and toggled to create a cone of D.O.T.S light where it is pointed. The Tier I D.O.T.S Projector will not work if Flashlights are turned off in custom difficulty settings
- Though the D.O.T.S Projector is an electronic equipment, only the Tier I D.O.T.S. Projector when activated and held will attract the ghost to the player during a hunt, and they are otherwise safe to hold onto
- D.O.T.S. Projector (Evidence)
- If the favourite room is small, consider placing the D.O.T.S Projector in a neighbouring room to aid visibility
- The Projector emits a substantial amount of light when placed. Investigators can use them for illumination, especially with a spare Projector or when D.O.T.S evidence can be safely crossed off. Note that the D.O.T.S projector light does not mitigate sanity drain
- The Tier II and III D.O.T.S Projectors can be placed on some ceilings in a few maps, which may allow the light to cover a room with much more consistency. This is especially useful for the tier III projector, whose sweeping motions and cone-shaped light area can cause the ghost to potentially leave its light prematurely depending on the ghost's and the projector's movement
- The green light emitted may obscure nearby fingerprints or footprints; look carefully or remove the projector when checking for ultraviolet evidence
- The tier III D.O.T.S Projector emits a constant motor whirring sound when active, which can both be quite annoying and interfere with the player's hearing when trying to listen for ghost activity. If you press Interact when pointing at the projector, it will stop in its current position, allowing it to operate in complete silence at the cost of covering a smaller area; interacting again will turn it off completely, and interacting a third time will turn it back on again

## EMF Reader

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/EMF_Reader

- Price: $45
- Maxamount: 2
- Starter: Yes
- Tier Ii Level: 18
- Tier Iii Level: 46

### Tier I
- Description: An EMF Meter with several modes for checking different interferences in the air. Sadly, it’s old and only one of them works, luckily, it’s the one we need for paranormal readings!
- Use: Switch this EMF meter on and scan anything that moves to see if you get a reading.; If the needle starts to move rapidly above a reading of 5, you’ve found some evidence!
- Stats: Accuracy: Low; Range (m): 1.7; Electronic

### Tier Ii
- Description: The trusty K2, it’s been used by paranormal investigators all over the world with consistent results.
- Use: The K2 meter has a larger range and five LED lights to display EMF readings, as well as an additional speaker module.; One light will turn on when you activate the device, two to four lights will emit for general interactions, and all five lights will glow when evidence has been found!
- Stats: Accuracy: Medium; Range (m): 2; Audio Indicator; Electronic

### Tier Iii
- Description: A custom-made device from Ghost Huntin’ Distribution. Capable of much more than just displaying EMF values on its large screen.
- Use: This EMF Reader can track up to three EMF spots at once. For each of them it will display the range, a directional display and the intensity of the reading.; Once an EMF spot fades away, the remaining readings will be pushed up in the list on the screen, so that the newest one is always at the bottom.; Each EMF reading will also play a beeping sound periodically, a higher pitch note relates to a higher intensity of EMF!
- Stats: Accuracy: High; Range (m): 3.5; Audio Indicator; Display Screen; Distance Indicator; Directional Indicator; Electronic

### Mechanics
- The reader can be toggled on to begin reading any emissions. The device's effective range, as well as how the reading is displayed, is dependent on the tier of the EMF Reader
- Holding an active EMF reader within 7.5 metres of a ghost (or 2.5 metres for a Yokai) during a hunt on the same floor will attract the ghost. During hunts or when the ghost manifests, EMF readers within 10 metres of the ghost (or higher for a Raiju) will malfunction and display random values between level 1 and 4
- The Tier I EMF Reader is an old GheistField Meter. It is reminiscent of an old Geiger counter, and has a 1.7 metre range at which it can pick up readings. When in range of an EMF reading, it will begin clicking and the dial will move to the rough area corresponding to the value of the EMF source, though the dial will sway up or down a bit due to its age
- When EMF Level 5 is detected, the dial will quickly shoot all the way to the right, and sway a lot less down, staying at and far past the level 5 mark (staying at and below does not count)
- The Tier II EMF Reader is the K2 Meter. The K2 is a small black box adorned with five colored LED lights, each one indicating a different EMF reading. The K2 has an effective range of 2 metres. When detecting an EMF emission, the reader will light up various numbers of its LEDs, accompanied with a pitch associated with the measured reading's level. If multiple EMF emissions are in range, the one with the highest EMF level will be indicated by the reader, though some fine positioning may be able to change which reading its being given out
- When EMF Level 5 is detected, all five lights will be illuminated, and it will make a very high pitch tone
- The Tier III EMF Reader is the EMF ParaMeter. It is similar in design to a PDA, with a blocky black plastic shell and a large center screen. It has a 3.5 meter range. It is able to read and track up to three interactions at once, giving the reading's EMF level by filling in dashes, and additionally reads out how far away that the reading occurred from the device (in meters) as well as the direction at which the interaction happened, allowing for the holder to quickly track down where the EMF emission is sourced from
- When detecting something that is not EMF Level 5, it will create a beeping sound. When detecting EMF Level 5, all dashes will fill and a unique beeping noise that is higher pitched will play

## Firelight

Source: https://phasmophobia.fandom.com/wiki/Firelight

- Price: $15
- Maxamount: 4
- Starter: No
- Tier I Level: 12
- Tier Ii Level: 37
- Tier Iii Level: 75

### Tier I
- Description: An old, rusting candleholder supporting a small candle, hopefully, it will last long enough if there’s no other light. Don’t forget something to light it with!
- Use: This small candle will provide light and some sanity loss prevention, but only for a while, the wax burns down quite fast!
- Stats: Range (m): 2; Duration (min): 5; Sanity Drain Reduction (%): 33; Placeable; Consumable

### Tier Ii
- Description: A candelabra with three tall candles attached. The ornate metal and higher-quality candle wax make for a much more reliable sanity saver!
- Use: With three candles instead of one, the light provided is much brighter, longer range and lasts longer.; Additionally, this Firelight doesn’t need to be held to gain its effects, so you can place it and stand nearby to still receive the sanity benefits!
- Stats: Range (m): 2; Duration (min): 10; Sanity Drain Reduction (%): 50; Placeable; Consumable

### Tier Iii
- Description: It may look old, but this gasoline fuelled lantern is the most reliable firelight we’ve found, it can last for hours on end.
- Use: This lantern will last for the entirety of a contract, so there's no worry of it going out when you need it most. Just like its predecessor you can stand nearby to gain its effects.; Lastly, it’s waterproof so it even works in the rain!
- Stats: Range (m): 2; Sanity Drain Reduction (%): 66; Waterproof; Placeable

### Mechanics
- Players can light a firelight held in their hand by pressing Primary Use if they have an igniter in their inventory. A firelight placed down can be lit while holding an igniter and pressing Secondary Use. One can also hold a lit firelight, aim at an unlit firelight, and use the Secondary Use key
- An unorthodox way is to have the wick of one lit and one unlit firelight to collide (this may need velocity)
- Firelights can be placed down on surfaces. Holding the placement key will show a translucent highlight of the firelight. This will place the firelight upright relative to the surface. Firelights can topple/fall over when placed on a slope or vertically
- When a player is within 2 metres of a lit firelight, it will slow passive sanity drain that results from being in the dark, at a rate depending on the firelight's tier level. Lit firelights do not affect sanity drain from other sources such as ghost events or other players dying
- Ghosts can blow out firelights at any time, even during hunts, and this will create an EMF 2 reading (or EMF Level 5). When blown out, the firelight will be extinguished with a blowing "sigh" effect. A firelight will also extinguish by itself at the end of its duration which depends on its tier level. Tier I and Tier II firelights will produce smoke when blown out by the ghost or self-extinguishing. Contrary to fire sources, however, ghosts cannot light firelights up
- Like with fire sources, when a firelight is lit up for the first time, there will be a 30-second cooldown before the ghost can attempt to blow it out. After the end of that cooldown or on subsequent lightings, a new cooldown is set with a random duration between 20 and 80 seconds. The Onryo can bypass these cooldowns due to its hunt prevention mechanics
- Note that Tier III firelights will pause the current cooldown if they are turned off by the player or an Onryo's hunt attempt
- There is currently a bug where if a firelight is lit by a non-host player, it will be considered unlit by ghosts despite being visually lit. Consequently, ghosts will not blow it out and the Onryo will ignore it

### Mechanics
- When a video camera is picked up, the flip screen will open automatically. The video camera can be used handheld with its built-in display or placed on a flat surface or a tripod. The video camera will automatically start streaming its video feed to the computer when held outside the truck or placed
- Holding the placement key will show a translucent preview of the video camera and its facing direction, which can be adjusted while holding the placement key by moving the mouse left and right. Be aware that even though certain spots are shown as valid, the camera may tip over or fall due to the geometry or other objects placed on the surface
- When the ghost manifests during a ghost event or a hunt, it will cause interference on all nearby video cameras and distort the image on both the built-in display and the video feed. Cameras of higher Tier with a better image quality will be significantly less impacted by ghost interference, aiding players in observing or tracking the ghost during a ghost event or a hunt
- Ghosts may occasionally knock over placed down video cameras within a certain range, forcing players to actively tend to them. Placing a camera on a tripod will reduce the probability of the ghost knocking it over. The stability provided by the tripod will depend on its Tier
- Night vision can be toggled while holding the active video camera or via the computer keyboard. When night vision is turned on, any Ghost Orbs present can be seen through the video camera
- Holding the Use button while holding the active video camera will start a video recording. If a valid subject is captured, it will be recorded in the Media tab in the journal. Video recording can also be started from the computer in the truck by clicking on the Rec button
- The video camera's night vision function can be very useful when using a D.O.T.S Projector as the ghost's silhouette may be easier to see with night vision
- The video camera can also be used to maintain surveillance on areas such as hallways, rooms, and the fuse box. This can allow for easy viewing of certain stationary evidence like Fingerprints, Ghost Writing, EMF Level 5, salt, or D.O.T.S Projector. However, the video feed becomes extremely distorted whenever the ghost manifests during a Ghost Event or a hunt, making observation very difficult
